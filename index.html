Fundamental Rights

Fundamental Rights are the essential freedoms guaranteed by the Constitution of India, ensuring equality, freedom, justice, and protection against discrimination for every citizen. Explore your rights to know how they safeguard your dignity and empower your voice.
Preamble

The Preamble of the Indian Constitution serves as the guiding light of the nation, encapsulating the core values of Justice, Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity. It reflects the vision of a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic, setting the foundation for the rights and duties of every citizen.

Fun Facts

The Preamble was the last part of the Constitution to be adopted but is considered the soul of the document, summarizing its entire philosophy in just a few powerful lines.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What are Fundamental Rights?
      Fundamental Rights are basic human rights guaranteed by the Indian Constitution to all citizens, ensuring their personal freedom and equality.
  • How many Fundamental Rights are there?
      There are six Fundamental Rights enshrined in the Constitution of India.
  • Can Fundamental Rights be suspended?
      Yes, Fundamental Rights can be suspended during a state of emergency, except for the right to life and personal liberty.
  • What is the difference between Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles?
      Fundamental Rights are legally enforceable and protect individual freedoms, while Directive Principles are guidelines for creating social policies and are not legally enforceable.
  • What happens if someone's Fundamental Rights are violated?
      Individuals can approach the judiciary, particularly the Supreme Court or High Courts, to seek redress and enforcement of their Fundamental Rights.

  • What are Directive Principles of State Policy?
      They are guidelines set out in the Indian Constitution for the government to follow in making laws and policies to promote social and economic welfare.
  • How many Directive Principles are there in the Indian Constitution?
      There are 15 Directive Principles listed in Part IV of the Indian Constitution.
  • Are Directive Principles legally enforceable?
      No, Directive Principles are non-justiciable, meaning they are not enforceable by the courts, but they guide the government's policy-making.
  • Can Directive Principles be amended?
      Yes, Directive Principles can be amended by Parliament, similar to other parts of the Constitution.
  • How do Directive Principles differ from Fundamental Rights?
      Directive Principles are aimed at guiding the state in policy-making and achieving socio-economic justice, whereas Fundamental Rights are enforceable legal rights that protect individual freedoms.

  • What are Fundamental Duties in the Indian Constitution?
      Fundamental Duties are a set of moral obligations for Indian citizens, aimed at promoting a sense of responsibility and national commitment.
  • How many Fundamental Duties are listed in the Constitution?
      here are eleven Fundamental Duties enumerated in Article 51A of the Indian Constitution.
  • Are there any penalties for not following Fundamental Duties?
      No, there are no legal penalties for not adhering to Fundamental Duties; they are not legally enforceable but serve as ethical guidelines.
  • What is the purpose of Fundamental Duties?
      The purpose of Fundamental Duties is to encourage citizens to respect the Constitution, uphold democratic values, and contribute positively to society.
  • Can Fundamental Duties be amended?
      Yes, Fundamental Duties can be amended by Parliament through constitutional amendments.
