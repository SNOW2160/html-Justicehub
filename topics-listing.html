The union and its Territory

The Union and its territory define India's structure and geographical extent. Part I of the Constitution specifies that India includes States, Union Territories, and any acquired territories. It governs the distribution of power between the Union and States and outlines the process for altering state boundaries or creating new states, ensuring India's territorial integrity. This reflects the nation's unity in diversity, bringing together different regions under one framework.

Citizenship

Citizenship in India is governed by Part II of the Constitution. It outlines who is considered a citizen at the commencement of the Constitution and allows for laws governing the acquisition and termination of citizenship. It ensures rights and duties specific to citizens, reflecting India's inclusive approach while maintaining the integrity of the nation.

Fundamental Rights

Fundamental Rights are the essential freedoms guaranteed by the Constitution of India, ensuring equality, freedom, justice, and protection against discrimination for every citizen. Explore your rights to know how they safeguard your dignity and empower your voice.
